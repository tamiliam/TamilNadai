About Tamil Nadai

A collaborative platform for Tamil writing standards

Our Mission

Tamil Nadai is an open platform for documenting, discussing, and validating Tamil writing conventions. Our primary goal is to build a gold-standard evaluation dataset for testing how well Large Language Models (LLMs) handle Tamil writing — grounded in established standards and validated through scholarly collaboration.

The name தமிழ் நடை (Tamil Nadai) means "Tamil style" or "Tamil usage" — it refers to the accepted conventions of written Tamil, as distinct from grammar rules alone. Getting the நடை right is what makes Tamil text look and feel natural to a reader.

Why This Matters

Tamil currently has only 65 sentences in the IndicGEC benchmark — the most widely used grammar evaluation dataset for Indian languages. For a language with over 80 million speakers, this is wholly inadequate. LLMs like GPT, Gemini, and Claude consistently fail on exactly the prescriptive conventions our rules test — sandhi doubling, word joining, ஒரு vs ஓர் usage — yet there is no rigorous way to measure or compare their performance.

Tamil Nadai aims to produce ~500 expert-annotated sentence pairs (erroneous and corrected), each traced to a specific rule and page in an authoritative publication, organised into a 12-category error taxonomy. This dataset will be:

  • Published on HuggingFace for open access
  • The largest prescriptive Tamil grammar evaluation set in existence
  • Immediately usable to benchmark any LLM or NLP model on Tamil
  • A foundation for grammar checking tools, NLP training data, and teaching materials

Source Material

Tamil Nadai builds on two foundations. The first is the தமிழ் நடைக் கையேடு (Tamil Writing Style Guide) published by the Tamil Virtual University (tamilvu.org), an institution of the Government of Tamil Nadu — the most authoritative contemporary reference for Tamil writing conventions.

The second is a decade of work encoding these conventions into 85+ machine-readable grammar rules for LanguageTool, an open-source proofreading tool — the first systematic attempt to make Tamil writing conventions testable by software.

The rules are organised into three categories:

  • இலக்கண அமைப்பில் சொற்கள் — Word joining based on grammatical structure
  • தனிச் சொற்களை எழுதும் முறை — Conventions for writing individual words
  • சந்தி — Sandhi (euphonic combination rules)

How It Works

Each rule in the workbench is paired with example sentences — both correct and incorrect usages. These sentences go through a collaborative review process:

  1. A contributor adds an example sentence (manually or with AI assistance)
  2. Two independent reviewers evaluate the sentence
  3. If both reviewers accept it, the sentence is permanently recorded
  4. If either reviewer rejects it, the sentence is marked for revision

This two-reviewer system ensures quality and consensus before any sentence becomes part of the reference collection.

University Collaboration

Tamil Nadai is being developed as a collaborative research tool in partnership with the Department of Malaysian Languages and Applied Linguistics at the Faculty of Languages and Linguistics, Universiti Malaya (fll.um.edu.my). The department offers one of Malaysia's only Bachelor programmes in Tamil Language and Linguistics.

We welcome collaboration from other universities and language institutions. If your department works on Tamil linguistics, corpus development, or computational approaches to Tamil, we would be glad to have you involved.

Free Software

Tamil Nadai is Free Software, released under the GNU General Public License (GPL). This means:

  • Anyone can use, study, and modify the software
  • Modified versions must also be made freely available
  • The data and rules collected are a public good, not proprietary

We believe that language standards belong to the community that uses the language. No corporation or institution should be able to restrict access to the rules of how Tamil is written.

Background

This project builds on over a decade of work in Tamil language technology. The original effort began in 2014 with the creation of 85+ grammar checking rules for LanguageTool, an open-source proofreading tool. Those rules were the first systematic attempt to encode Tamil writing conventions into software that could check text automatically.

The same contributor led Tamil localisation efforts for Mozilla Firefox, OpenOffice, and LibreOffice — making these tools accessible to millions of Tamil speakers. Tamil Nadai is the next step: building a validated, peer-reviewed corpus of Tamil writing conventions that can power the next generation of Tamil language tools.
